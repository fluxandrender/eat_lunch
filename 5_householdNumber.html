Everyone that lives together and shares money is a household.

See examples    

Every household is different. Some people just live with close family. Other people live with grandparents, friends, or roommates. Here are some examples:

  • A mother and her two children.
  • A father, a mother, their child and two roommates.
  • A grandmother, her grandchild and a foster child.

Someone who rents a room in the house and doesn't share expenses with you is not a part of your household.
